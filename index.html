Why Cook at Home?

Cooking at home is not only healthier, but it's also a great way to save money and have fun. Whether you’re feeding yourself or others, knowing how to prepare a few basic meals is a skill worth learning.

Easy Recipes

1. Classic Scrambled Eggs

Crack two eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt, and whisk. Heat a pan, melt some butter, and pour in the eggs. Stir gently until set. Done in under 5 minutes!

2. One-Pot Pasta

Combine dry pasta, chopped vegetables, garlic, and broth in a pot. Boil until the pasta is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. Simple and delicious.

3. Banana Pancakes

Mash one banana and mix with two eggs. Cook small pancakes on a nonstick pan for a naturally sweet breakfast.

Kitchen Safety

Before you start cooking, it’s important to know a few safety basics to avoid accidents.

Avoiding Fires

Never leave hot pans unattended. Keep flammable items like paper towels away from the stove. Always turn pot handles inward.

Knife Safety

Always cut away from your body and use a cutting board. A sharp knife is safer than a dull one because it requires less force.

Tools You’ll Need

You don’t need a fancy kitchen to get started. Here are a few tools every beginner should have:

  • A good frying pan
  • Cutting board
  • Sharp knife
  • Mixing bowl
  • Measuring cups and spoons
